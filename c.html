The Three-Headed God · Supreme Ruler of Heaven, Earth & the Underworld

🌍 Domain & Nature

Triglav (meaning "Three-Headed") was a major deity worshipped by West Slavic tribes, especially in Pomerania (around Szczecin and Wolin). He is regarded as a supreme or high god, embodying the tripartite structure of the universe: heaven, earth, and the underworld. His three heads symbolize absolute dominion over these three realms, as well as governance over time — past, present, and future — and the three classical elements: air, earth, and fire.

Chroniclers such as Ebbo and Herbord described Triglav’s temple in Szczecin as a major cult center, where the golden statue of the three-headed god stood adorned with a golden diadem binding the heads together — a symbol of the unity of worlds.

⚙️ Attributes & Sacred Symbols

  • Three heads – omniscience, control over all levels of existence; the heads see every corner of the world, making Triglav the ultimate guardian and judge.
  • Blindfold or covered eyes – paradoxically, some accounts mention a veil over his eyes. This signifies that he deliberately ignores evil or mortal suffering, or that his divine vision is too powerful for human eyes to endure.
  • Black horse – a sacred black horse was kept at his temple for divination. Priests would lead the horse through a grid of spears; the animal's movements were interpreted as Triglav’s prophecies.
  • Spear or sword – in some depictions, each head wears a crown and the deity holds a weapon, representing warlike protection and authority.
  • Golden diadem – binding the three heads together, emphasizing the inseparable unity of the cosmic realms.
“The three-headed idol stood in the main shrine, covered with a golden veil, and its eyes were mysteriously covered — only priests could approach and interpret its silent will.”

🔱 Relations & Cosmic Role

  • Counterpart to Svarog or Perun – Triglav is often seen as a supreme sky god, equivalent to Svarog (the celestial smith) or Perun (the thunderer) in other Slavic traditions, but with a distinctive threefold nature.
  • Temple of Szczecin – the main cult center: walls inside and out were carved with images of warriors, beasts, and ritual scenes. The golden statue of Triglav was the focal point of tributes and sacrifices.
  • Sacrifices & rituals – worship involved frequent offerings of cattle, and occasionally prisoners of war. A sacred oak tree stood near the temple, where divinations were performed.
  • Mountain spirit & folk memory – Triglav’s name endures in Slovenia’s highest peak, Mount Triglav. Folk tales transformed the god into a three-headed giant or a mountain guardian, watching over the gateway to the otherworld.
  • Triad of supreme gods – for some tribes, Triglav, together with Svarog (heaven) and Dazhbog (sun), formed a sacred trinity representing cosmic order.

In later folklore, Triglav became associated with the number three’s sacred power: three realms, three times, three elements — a pattern that echoes through Slavic ritual and symbol.

🏛️ Worship & Christian Suppression

Triglav’s cult was especially strong among the Lutici and Pomeranian Slavs. The temple in Szczecin (Stettin) was destroyed during the Christianization campaigns of the 12th century, led by Bishop Otto of Bamberg. According to medieval records, the statue of Triglav was demolished, but local resistance remained fierce — the god’s black horse and sacred groves were considered sources of oracular power.

Despite the destruction, the memory of the three-headed god survived in folk art, oral tales, and even in the toponymy of the Alps. Modern Rodnovery (Slavic native faith) revival honors Triglav as a guardian of cosmic law and the unity of worlds.

✨ Triglav’s essence: absolute sight, triune power, the knot that ties heaven, earth, and the underworld into a single unbreakable chain. He neither sleeps nor blinks — his three faces watch the past, the present, and what lies beyond.

📜 Based on medieval chronicles (Ebbo, Herbord, Saxo Grammaticus) and Slavic comparative mythology.
