🌿 SŁAWA — Slavic Paganism 🌲

Ancient gods, sacred symbols, and timeless lore from the forests, rivers & skies of the old Slavs.

⚡ Perun
God of Thunder, Lightning & Warriors

Lore: Perun is the supreme Slavic god of sky, thunder, and sacred oak trees. He wields a mighty axe or fiery arrows, battling the serpent Veles to maintain cosmic order. Slavic princes swore oaths by Perun's name.

Symbolism: The hexafoil / thunder mark (Gromoviti znak) and the axe represent his destructive yet purifying power over evil.

🌙 Veles
God of Underworld, Magic, Cattle & Poetry

Lore: Veles, the shape‑shifting serpent or bear, rules the watery depths of Nav (the afterlife). He is the eternal rival of Perun, protector of merchants, musicians, and livestock. Offerings were left at crossroads.

Symbolism: Horns, knots, and twisted spirals reflect his connection to the labyrinthine underworld, wisdom, and raw nature.

🌾 Mokosh
Goddess of Fertility, Earth, Women & Fate

Lore: Mokosh, the great mother, spins the threads of life and destiny. She is the moist earth, patroness of weaving, childbirth, and harvest. Women prayed to her for protection and abundance.

Symbolism: Crossed spindles, diamond patterns, and fertile lozenge motifs represent her dominion over life-giving forces.

☀️ Dazhbog
Sun God, Giver of Warmth & Abundance

Lore: Dazhbog, the shining sun‑deity, rides his chariot across the heavens. He is considered the ancestor of the Rus' people (the "Sun Tsar"). His light brings life, joy and wealth to the righteous.

Symbolism: The solar wheel (kolovrat) and eight‑rayed star represent never‑ending celestial fire and cyclical rebirth.

🌸 Lada
Goddess of Spring, Love & Harmony

Lore: Lada, the great goddess of beauty and marriage, brings the spring season and protects lovers. Her rites were celebrated with singing, flower wreaths and fire‑jumping during Kupala night.

Symbolism: The star‑flower, birch tree, and intertwined rings embody grace, fertility, and marital bliss.

⚒️ Svarog
Celestial Smith, Father of the Gods

Lore: Svarog, the divine blacksmith, forged the sun and the earthly order. He taught humanity to work metal and create laws. After his creation, the world was governed by his sons Dazhbog and Svarozhich.

Symbolism: Hammer, anvil, and heavenly fire represent creation, law, and the sacred craft of smithcraft.

🌲 Sacred Traditions & Rodnovery 🌲

✨ Holy places: Temples (kapishche) were often located in groves or near springs. Idols (kapy) carved from wood represented the deities.

🔥 Kupala Night: Midsummer celebration with fire‑jumping, floating wreaths, and searching for the mythical fern flower.

🌾 Harvest rites: Offerings of bread, honey, and grain to Mokosh and Dazhbog. Ancestor veneration was central to Slavic belief (Dziady).

🐍 Cosmic battle: Perun and Veles eternally struggle – storms clash with the underworld, bringing rain and renewal to the land.

📜 Modern Rodnovery: Slavic native faith revival honors these ancient gods, seasonal circles (Kolo Goda), and traditional symbols as heritage and spiritual path.

🔱 The Slavic pagan worldview honors nature, ancestors, and the eternal rhythm of the sun, earth, and sky. All SVG symbols are stylized representations inspired by archaeological findings & folk art. 🔱
