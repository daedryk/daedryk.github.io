🌿 SLOVANSKI BOGOVI 🌿

Slavic Deities · Kinship · Sacred Symbols & Attributes
⚡ Perun God of Thunder, Lightning & War

⚡ Attributes: Axe / hammer, thunderbolts, oak tree, fire, eagle.

🔹 Symbols: Gromoviti znak (thunder mark), hexafoil, perunika (iris flower).

👥 Relations: Son of Svarog (celestial smith). Eternal rival of Veles. Father of Yarilo (spring deity) in some myths. Leader of the pantheon.
🐍 Veles God of Underworld, Magic, Cattle & Poetry

🐉 Attributes: Horns, serpent / dragon, bear, cattle, wealth, music, magic, water.

🔹 Symbols: Spiral, knot, horned figure, willow tree.

👥 Relations: Eternal adversary of Perun (cosmic balance). Opposes the sky god; protector of merchants and shamans. Often associated with Chernobog's domain.
⚒️ Svarog Celestial Smith, Father of Gods

🔥 Attributes: Hammer, anvil, forge, heavenly fire, law, creation, blacksmithing.

🔹 Symbols: Alatyr stone (sacred anvil), flame wheel, the smith's cross.

👥 Relations: Father of Dazhbog (sun) and Svarozhich (fire). Husband of Lada? (some traditions). Forged the sun and the world order.
☀️ Dazhbog Sun God, Giver of Wealth & Light

🌞 Attributes: Solar chariot, golden radiance, white horse, fire, wealth, justice.

🔹 Symbols: Kolovrat (eight-spoked solar wheel), swastika (solar sign), circle with rays.

👥 Relations: Son of Svarog. Consort / sister often Zhiva (goddess of life). Grandfather of the Slavic people (mythical ancestor).
🌾 Mokosh Great Mother, Goddess of Earth & Fate

🧵 Attributes: Spindle, distaff, moist earth, fertility, weaving, women's destiny, rain.

🔹 Symbols: Diamond (lozenge), crossed spindles, birch tree, female figurines.

👥 Relations: Wife of Perun in some accounts, or independent. Mother of Yarilo and the spirits of fate (Rozhanitsy). Associated with matron goddess.
🌸 Lada Goddess of Love, Beauty & Spring

💐 Attributes: Flower wreath, cuckoo, birch, apple blossom, spring water, harmony.

🔹 Symbols: Seven-pointed star, ring, rose, floral patterns.

👥 Relations: Consort of Svarog (or Lado as male counterpart). Mother of Lel and Polel (twin gods of love and marriage). Protector of couples.
❄️ Morena Goddess of Death, Winter & Night

🌨️ Attributes: Frost, darkness, black horse, sickle, frozen waters, nightmare.

🔹 Symbols: Black sun, broken wheel, willow branch (funerary), sickle moon.

👥 Relations: Sister of Živa (life) and Lada. Daughter of Chernobog? Sometimes consort of Veles. Rival of Yarilo (spring god). Drowned each spring in rituals.
🌾 Yarilo God of Spring, Fertility & Passion

🌱 Attributes: White horse, wreath of wheat, flowering staff, youth, raw fertility, sun.

🔹 Symbols: Five-petal flower, ear of grain, horn of plenty.

👥 Relations: Son of Perun and Mokosh (or Dazhbog). Lover of Lada. Dies and reborn each year; defeats Morena to begin spring.
🔥 Svarozhich God of Hearth Fire & Sacred Flame

🏠 Attributes: Sacred fire, hearth, smith's spark, purification, altar flame.

🔹 Symbols: Flame tongue, cross in circle (fire cross), burning log.

👥 Relations: Son of Svarog. Brother of Dazhbog. Honored as domestic fire spirit; sometimes seen as aspect of Perun.
🌿 Živa (Żywie) Goddess of Life, Health & Vitality

💚 Attributes: Cuckoo bird, water of life, green branches, healing herbs, renewal.

🔹 Symbols: Cuckoo, leaf wreath, flowing water, the living knot.

👥 Relations: Sister of Morena (death) and Lada. Daughter of Svarog? She resurrects nature after winter. Companion of Dazhbog.
🌑 Chernobog God of Darkness, Chaos & Misfortune

🖤 Attributes: Black, curse, night, evil eye, ruin, trickery.

🔹 Symbols: Black sun (obverse), inverted star, skull.

👥 Relations: Antagonist to Belobog (white god) in dualistic belief. Father of Morena? Often allied with Veles' darker aspects.

🔱 Cosmic structure: Svarog (smith) → Dazhbog (sun) & Svarozhich (fire). Perun (thunder) battles Veles (underworld) to maintain order — a cycle of storm and calm. Mokosh (earth mother) weaves fate alongside Lada (love) and Živa (life). Seasonal death of Morena and rebirth through Yarilo reflects agricultural belief. Slavic pantheon emphasizes duality: sky/earth, light/dark, fire/water.
